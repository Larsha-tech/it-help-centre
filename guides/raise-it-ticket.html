How to Raise an IT Support Ticket

🎫 System: GLPI ⏱ Takes about 5 minutes 📅 Updated 2025
What is a support ticket? A ticket is how you officially tell IT about a problem or request. It creates a record so nothing gets lost and you can track progress.
1

Open the GLPI Login Page

Open your web browser and go to the GLPI helpdesk portal. Ask IT for the exact URL if you don't have it bookmarked.

📷

Enter your username and password, then click Sign In.

Tip: Use your normal Windows/email login credentials unless IT has told you otherwise.
2

Your Home Screen

After logging in you'll see your dashboard. Any tickets you've raised before are listed here with their current status:

StatusWhat it means
NewTicket received, not yet picked up
In ProgressIT is actively working on it
PendingWaiting for more info from you
SolvedIssue has been resolved
📷
Screenshot: GLPI home / ticket list
3

Click "Create a Ticket"

Look for the + Create a Ticket button — usually in the top menu or on your dashboard — and click it.

📷
Screenshot: Create a Ticket button highlighted

This opens the new ticket form.

4

Fill In the Ticket Details

Complete each field as described below. Fields marked with a red asterisk (*) are required.

Type *

Choose Incident if something is broken or not working. Choose Request if you need something new (software, access, equipment).

Category *

Pick the category that best matches your issue (e.g. Hardware, Software, Network, Account). If unsure, leave it on the default — IT will reassign it.

Urgency

How urgently do you need this fixed? Be honest — marking everything as "Very High" slows down response for everyone.

Associated Elements

If the issue is related to a specific device or asset registered in GLPI, you can link it here. This is optional.

Watchers

Add your manager or a colleague here if they should receive updates on this ticket. Optional.

Title *

Write a short, clear summary of the problem.

✅ Good: "Laptop won't connect to office Wi-Fi since this morning"
❌ Vague: "Wi-Fi not working"
Description *

Explain the issue in detail. Include: what you were doing when it happened, any error messages you saw, and what you've already tried.

I was trying to print a document from my laptop (Windows 11, HP EliteBook) at 9 am.
The printer shows as offline even though it was working yesterday.
I've restarted both the laptop and the printer but the problem continues.
Add a File

Attach a screenshot of any error messages — this helps IT fix your issue faster. Click Add a file and select the image from your computer.

📷
Screenshot: Completed ticket form
5

Submit the Ticket

Once you've filled in all the required fields, click the Submit button at the bottom of the form.

📷
Screenshot: Submit button

You'll be taken to your new ticket's page where you can see its ID number and current status. You'll also receive a confirmation email.

Keep your ticket ID safe. If you need to follow up with IT by phone or email, quoting the ticket ID (e.g. #1042) means IT can find your case instantly.

✅ What happens next?

  • IT will review your ticket and assign it to the right person.
  • You'll receive email updates when the status changes.
  • If IT needs more info, they'll add a comment — you'll get an email and can reply directly in GLPI.
  • Once solved, you may be asked to confirm the issue is fixed before the ticket is closed.
Need help raising a ticket? Contact IT on Ext. 101 or email it@houseofbluebeans.com
