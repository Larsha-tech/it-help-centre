How to Raise an IT Support Ticket

Introduction

If you're experiencing a technical issue — such as a computer not working, software problems, internet connectivity issues, or any other IT-related concern — you can report it quickly using our IT support system called GLPI. This guide walks you through how to log in and submit a support request so our IT team can assist you as soon as possible.

No technical knowledge is required. Just follow the steps below.

Step 1 — Open the GLPI Login Page

Link: https://ticket.hobb-dashboard.com

You will be taken to the GLPI login page — a white card with the GLPI logo at the top, and fields for your login and password.

What to do:

  1. In the Login field, enter your official company email address (e.g. yourname@houseofbluebeans.com).
  2. In the Password field, enter your system password — the same password you use to log in to your work computer.
  3. Leave the Login source as "GLPI internal database" — do not change this.
  4. Optionally tick "Remember me" if you are on your personal work computer (avoid this on shared machines).
  5. Click the yellow Sign in button.
💡 Tip: If you've forgotten your password, click the "Forgotten password?" link on the login page and follow the instructions, or contact the IT team directly.

Step 2 — Your Home Screen

After logging in you will see your ticket dashboard. Any tickets you have raised before are listed here with their current status.

StatusWhat it means

Processing (assigned)An IT team member has been assigned to it

Step 3 — Click "+ Create a ticket"

Click the + Create a ticket button at the top right of your dashboard, or use the link in the left-hand menu. This opens the new ticket form.

Step 4 — Fill In the Ticket Details

Complete each field in the form as described below. Fields marked with a red asterisk (*) are required.

  1. Type — Choose Incident if something is broken or not working. Choose Request if you need something new (software, access, or equipment).
  2. Category — Pick the category that best matches your issue (e.g. Hardware, Software, Network, Account). If unsure, leave it blank — IT will assign the right category.
  3. Urgency — Default is Medium. Only raise this if your work is completely blocked. Marking everything urgent slows response times for everyone.
  4. Associated Elements — If the issue relates to a specific device registered in GLPI, link it here using the + button. This field is optional.
  5. Watchers — Add a colleague or manager here if they should receive updates on this ticket. Optional.
  6. Title * — Write a short, clear one-line summary of the problem.
    ✅ Good: "Laptop won't connect to office Wi-Fi since this morning"
    ❌ Vague: "Wi-Fi not working"
  7. Description * — Explain the issue in detail. Include: what you were doing when it happened, any error messages you saw, and what you've already tried.

    Example: "I was trying to print a document from my HP EliteBook (Windows 11) at 9 am. The printer shows as offline even though it worked yesterday. I've restarted the laptop and printer but the problem continues."
  8. File Attachment — Drag and drop a screenshot of any error message, or click Choose files. Maximum file size: 2 MB. This helps IT diagnose the issue faster.
💡 Tip: The more detail you give in the description, the faster IT can resolve your issue — good descriptions cut out multiple back-and-forth messages.

Step 5 — Submit the Ticket

Once all required fields are filled in, click the + Submit message button at the bottom of the form.

You'll be taken to your new ticket's page showing its ID number and current status. You'll also receive a confirmation email.

💡 Keep your ticket ID. If you follow up with IT by phone or email, quoting the ticket ID (e.g. #1042) lets IT find your case instantly.

What Happens Next?

  • IT will review and assign your ticket to the right person.
  • You'll receive email updates whenever the status changes.
  • If IT needs more information, they'll add a comment — you can reply directly in GLPI or by email.
  • Once solved, confirm the issue is fixed and the ticket will be closed.
