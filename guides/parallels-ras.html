How to Access Your Office PC Remotely

Introduction

Once your IT onboarding is complete, you will be assigned a dedicated office PC. Using Parallels RAS Client, you can securely connect to that PC from your home computer — just as if you were sitting at your desk in the office.

This guide walks you through downloading, installing, and connecting in under 10 minutes. No technical experience is needed.

Before you start: Make sure you have your username and password provided by IT, and your office PC name (e.g. HBB-R1-022). These are sent to you during onboarding.

Step 1 — Download the Parallels RAS Client

On your home computer, open a web browser and go to the Parallels RAS download page:

Link: https://www.parallels.com/products/ras/download/client/

  1. Make sure Version 21 (Latest version) is selected in the dropdown.
  2. Click the Windows tab.
  3. Under MSI installers, click Parallels Client (Windows) 64-bit Setup to download the installer.

Step 2 — Install the Parallels RAS Client

Once the download is complete, open the installer file and follow the setup wizard:

  1. On the Welcome screen, click Next.
  2. Read the license agreement, tick "I accept the terms in the License Agreement", then click Next.
  3. Leave the Destination Folder as the default (C:\Program Files\Parallels\Client\) and click Next.
  4. On the Shortcuts screen, leave the defaults checked and click Next.
  5. On the Shadowing screen, select "Do not allow shadowing" and click Next.
  6. On the SSO Installation screen, select "Do not install Single Sign-On component" and click Next.
  7. Click Install and wait for the installation to finish.
  8. Once complete, leave "Launch Parallels Client" ticked and click Finish.

Step 3 — Set Up the Connection to the Office

Parallels Client will open automatically after installation. A New Connection dialog will appear.

  1. In the server address field, type exactly: Parallels.houseofbluebeans.in
  2. Click Next.

Step 4 — Set the Port and Enter Your Credentials

On the next screen you will see the server and connection mode. Click Advanced settings > at the bottom left — the Connection Properties window will open. It has two sections to fill in:

Connection settings (top section):

  1. The Primary connection will already show Parallels.houseofbluebeans.in — leave it as is.
  2. Check the Port field — if it shows 443, change it to 4443.

Log in (bottom section):

  1. Set Authentication type to Credentials.
  2. In the Username field, enter the username provided by IT during onboarding.
  3. In the Password field, enter your password. Tick Save password if this is your personal work computer.
  4. In the Domain field, enter hobb.com.

Once all fields are filled, click OK, then click Create.

💡 Important: The port must be 4443 and the domain must be hobb.com — the connection will fail without these. If you have forgotten your username or password, contact IT directly.

Step 5 — Connect to Your Office PC

After logging in, you will see a screen showing the desktops available to you. Your assigned office PC will appear here by name (e.g. HBB-R1-022).

  1. Find your PC by its name — IT will have told you this during onboarding.
  2. Double-click the icon to connect.
  3. Your office desktop will open in a full window — you are now working on your office PC remotely.
💡 Tip: Your connection is saved — next time you open Parallels Client, just double-click your PC straight away. You do not need to re-enter the server or port.

Need Additional Software?

If you need any software installed on your office PC — such as a specific application for your work — you must raise a support ticket with IT. Do not attempt to install software yourself.

Troubleshooting

  • Cannot connect / connection refused — Double-check the server is Parallels.houseofbluebeans.in and the port is 4443.
  • Wrong username, password, or domain — Make sure the domain is hobb.com. Contact IT to reset your credentials if needed.
  • My PC does not appear in the list — Your PC may not be switched on at the office. Contact IT to check.
  • Screen is slow or laggy — This is usually caused by a slow home internet connection. Try connecting via a wired ethernet cable instead of Wi-Fi.
  • Skipped Advanced Settings by mistake — If you clicked through without setting the port or credentials, you can still fix it. In the Parallels Client, right-click Parallels.houseofbluebeans.in listed under Connections and select Connection Properties… from the menu. This opens the same dialog — update the port to 4443, fill in your username, password, and domain (hobb.com), then click OK and try connecting again.
